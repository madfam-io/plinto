## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
This Information Security Policy establishes the framework for protecting Janua's information assets, customer data, and technology infrastructure. It ensures compliance with SOC2 Trust Services Criteria and regulatory requirements while supporting business objectives.

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to:
- All Janua employees, contractors, and third parties
- All information systems, applications, and infrastructure
- All data processed, stored, or transmitted by Janua
- All business operations and customer services

### 1.3 Policy Statement
Janua is committed to maintaining the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets through comprehensive security controls, risk management, and continuous improvement.

## 2. Information Security Objectives

### 2.1 Primary Objectives
- **Confidentiality**: Prevent unauthorized disclosure of sensitive information
- **Integrity**: Ensure accuracy and completeness of data and systems
- **Availability**: Maintain accessible and reliable systems and services
- **Compliance**: Meet all applicable legal and regulatory requirements
- **Customer Trust**: Protect customer data and maintain service reliability

### 2.2 Business Alignment
Information security supports business objectives by:
- Enabling secure digital transformation
- Protecting competitive advantages
- Maintaining customer trust and satisfaction
- Ensuring regulatory compliance
- Supporting operational efficiency

## 4. Risk Management Framework

### 4.1 Risk Assessment Process
1. **Asset Identification**: Catalog all information assets
2. **Threat Analysis**: Identify potential security threats
3. **Vulnerability Assessment**: Evaluate system weaknesses
4. **Risk Calculation**: Assess likelihood and impact
5. **Risk Treatment**: Implement appropriate controls

### 4.2 Risk Categories
- **Technical Risks**: System vulnerabilities, cyber attacks
- **Operational Risks**: Process failures, human error
- **Compliance Risks**: Regulatory violations, audit findings
- **Strategic Risks**: Business disruption, reputation damage

### 4.3 Risk Tolerance
- **Critical Assets**: Zero tolerance for compromise
- **Sensitive Data**: Low tolerance with strict controls
- **Business Operations**: Moderate tolerance with monitoring
- **Public Information**: Standard protective measures

## 6. Data Classification and Handling

### 6.1 Data Classification Levels

#### 6.1.1 Public
- **Definition**: Information intended for public disclosure
- **Examples**: Marketing materials, public documentation
- **Protection**: Standard protective measures
- **Handling**: No special restrictions

#### 6.1.2 Internal
- **Definition**: Information for internal business use
- **Examples**: Policies, procedures, internal communications
- **Protection**: Access controls and monitoring
- **Handling**: Authorized personnel only

#### 6.1.3 Confidential
- **Definition**: Sensitive business information
- **Examples**: Financial data, strategic plans, customer lists
- **Protection**: Encryption and strict access controls
- **Handling**: Need-to-know basis with approval

#### 6.1.4 Restricted
- **Definition**: Highly sensitive information
- **Examples**: Customer authentication data, security keys
- **Protection**: Advanced encryption and monitoring
- **Handling**: Minimal access with logging and approval

### 6.2 Handling Requirements
- Data must be classified upon creation or acquisition
- Protection measures must match classification level
- Access controls enforced based on classification
- Secure transmission and storage required
- Proper disposal procedures followed

## 7. Access Control Requirements

### 7.1 User Access Management
- Principle of least privilege enforced
- Role-based access control implemented
- Regular access reviews conducted
- Prompt access revocation upon termination
- Multi-factor authentication required

### 7.2 Privileged Access
- Elevated privileges granted only when necessary
- Additional approval required for privileged access
- Enhanced monitoring of privileged activities
- Regular review of privileged access rights
- Secure management of administrative accounts

### 7.3 Authentication Standards
- Strong password requirements enforced
- Multi-factor authentication mandatory
- Password managers recommended
- Regular password rotation required
- Account lockout after failed attempts

## 8. Incident Response

### 8.1 Incident Classification
- **Critical**: Immediate threat to operations or data
- **High**: Significant impact on systems or data
- **Medium**: Moderate impact with containment possible
- **Low**: Minor impact with standard procedures

### 8.2 Response Procedures
1. **Detection**: Identify and report incidents
2. **Classification**: Assess severity and impact
3. **Containment**: Isolate and limit damage
4. **Investigation**: Analyze root cause and scope
5. **Recovery**: Restore normal operations
6. **Lessons Learned**: Document and improve

### 8.3 Communication Requirements
- Immediate notification of security team
- Executive notification for critical incidents
- Customer notification as legally required
- Regulatory notification within timeframes
- Public communication as appropriate
